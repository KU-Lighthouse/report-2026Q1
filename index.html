UCPH Lighthouse enhances innovation and entrepreneurship across the university. We foster ideas and solutions for the benefit of people, society and the environment. This report covers UCPH Lighthouse's three activity tracks: Nurture, Engage and Empower. Q1 2026 is the first quarter with the new KPI framework.
Executive Summary

Q1 2026 — Key Learnings from UCPH Lighthouse

Q1 2026 is the first quarter with the new KPI framework — comparisons to 2025 should be read with this context in mind.

Curricular capacity building and student outreach — Lighthouse content is being embedded in curricular courses, but faculty breadth is not yet there. 414 students in Lighthouse-supported curricular courses, with a strong lead from Science followed by Humanities. Health is underway but at 19% of its 2025 level. Social Sciences, Law and Theology are not yet represented in Lighthouse-supported courses. 102 educators in capacity building activities is an important first step towards embedding innovation more broadly across faculties. 5 out of 6 faculties are represented in student awareness activities and leads, indicating a student pipeline across disciplines in extracurricular activities.
Active Engagement — Indicated growth in community and programmes, but 70% of participants are from outside UCPH. 277 participants accepted across 5 programmes — primarily students, with SPARK Denmark as the exception. Gender balance is healthy across programmes (48% female). 70% of participants come from outside UCPH — a pattern that should be strategically addressed for the right balance in light of the ambition for broader participation across faculties and disciplines. 18 events with 409 sign-ups — the three largest events accounted for 58% of all reach, which raises questions about the format and reach of smaller events.
Research Commercialisation — Strong start across early pipeline stages; faculty breadth is growing. IDFs filed, PoC applications received and PoC projects funded are all well underway relative to 2025 — InnoExplore has funded 4 projects against 5 for all of 2025. Health and Science still dominate the overall pipeline, but Humanities (8 leads, 4 PoC) and Law (1 PoC) show signs of broader engagement across disciplines. Licences and spin-outs are low — seasonality is expected, but the trajectory deserves attention at Q2.

Educators in Capacity Building Activities

Scaling innovation capacities through educators — new activity

Counts educators participating in Lighthouse-supported innovation or entrepreneurship capacity building activities. This is an amplifier for curriculum reach: trained educators carry the content into their own courses independently.

Educators in capacity building
102
Q1 2026 — first data point
Why this matters alongside curriculum numbers
Where the curriculum indicators count students reached directly by Lighthouse, this indicator captures the indirect reach — educators who take Lighthouse content and deliver it independently in their own courses. 102 educators in a single quarter is a meaningful number: even if each reaches 20 students, that is a potential 2,000 additional students not captured elsewhere in the data.
Data recommendation: Start tracking faculty and new vs. recurring split. This will allow the team to see whether capacity building is deepening engagement with already-active faculties or broadening into new ones — a key strategic question as curriculum reach varies significantly across UCPH.

The platform is growing. Student users grew by 112 (+22%) and student startup-projects by 56 (+16%) in Q1 2026. Both metrics show consistent forward momentum entering 2026. 33% of platform users (208 of 631) are not from UCPH.
Data recommendation: The indicators are based on registered users on the platform, without clear differentiation between active and inactive users and academic level (e.g. students, researchers). As a result, the indicator names "Student users" and "Student startup-projects" may be somewhat misleading and may be overcounted. Recommendation: consider auditing the platform data to establish a standard for data collection and analysis — before using these numbers for strategic decision-making. A CVR data collection methodology should also be established.
